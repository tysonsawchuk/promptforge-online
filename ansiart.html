⌛ ANSI/ASCII Art: A Quick History & How-To

      

        What is ANSI/ASCII Art?

        
    
          
  • ASCII Art uses keyboard characters (letters, numbers, punctuation) to make pictures, logos, and memes—dating back to 1960s printers and BBSes.
    • 
          
  • ANSI Art adds color, animation, and wild text tricks—exploded in the 1980s/90s thanks to PC BBSes, .NFO files, and cracktros.
    • 
          
  • From dial-up hacker boards to today’s memes, ASCII/ANSI lives on! Most emojis and even Discord memes started this way.
    • 
        

        How To Make It (in 2025):

        
    
          
  • Use the “Make Art” tool above for instant results.
    • 
          
  • Try online tools: TAAG, ascii-generator.site.
    • 
          
  • Hand-draw in a code editor (use “Fira Mono” or “Consolas” font, disable word wrap for best effect).
    • 
          
  • Steal inspiration from the classics above, then remix with your own flavor!
    • 
        

      

      

        Why Save ANSI/ASCII? This artform is pure code, *no copyright, no spyware, no “NFTs”—just imagination and keyboard power. PromptForge aims to save, share, and remix it forever.

    💡 Help, Troubleshooting & FAQ — Everything You Need to Become an ANSI/ASCII Pro!
  

  


    

      
🎨 What is ANSI/ASCII Art?

      

        ANSI/ASCII art is computer art made only with text and keyboard symbols. It can be static, animated, or even interactive. ANSI uses color and box-drawing, ASCII is pure text. Both are part of internet & hacker history, seen on BBSes, cracktros, old demos, games, and now the modern web!
      

    


    

      
🖥️ How Do I View ANSI/ASCII Art Correctly?

      
    
        
  • Web: This site auto-renders everything! For offline: use Chrome, Firefox, Edge, or Safari—zoom to 100% for best look.
    • 
        
  • Windows: Download Ansilove (free), or use Notepad++ (set font to 'Terminal' or 'Fira Mono').
    • 
        
  • Mac: Use TextEdit (set plain text mode) or iTerm2 for colors.
    • 
        
  • Linux: Try cat, less, nano, or ansilove.
    • 
        
  • Mobile: Best with our site! Offline: try ANSI/ASCII Viewer for Android.
    • 
      

    


    

      
⚙️ Why Does My Art Look Wrong / Spaced Out?

      
    
        
  • Always use a **monospaced font** (“Fira Mono”, “Consolas”, “Courier”, “Terminal”).
    • 
        
  • Zoom to 100% or “Actual Size.”
    • 
        
  • If colors are missing, try Chrome or Firefox, or upload to our site for instant fix!
    • 
        
  • On Mac/Windows, don’t use Word or Pages! Stick to Notepad, Notepad++, or plain-text editors.
    • 
      

    


    

      
📲 How Do I Download, Save, or Print Art?

      
    
        
  • Download: Use the download button in the gallery, or right-click and “Save As”.
    • 
        
  • Save: Save as .txt, .ans, or .nfo (plain text format). Keep filenames short and no spaces.
    • 
        
  • Print: Open in Notepad (Windows) or TextEdit (Mac) and print. Or use “Print to PDF” for digital artbooks!
    • 
      

    


    

      
🔊 My Animation/Sound/Share Feature Isn’t Working?

      
    
        
  • Animations need Chrome, Firefox, Edge, or Safari. Refresh if stuck.
    • 
        
  • Sound & TTS: Your browser/device must allow auto-play. Try clicking anywhere first to “unlock” sound.
    • 
        
  • Some features may be desktop-only or need permissions. See help in each tool.
    • 
      

    


    

      
🖌️ How Do I Make My Own ANSI/ASCII Art?

      
    
        
  • Simple: Use our built-in editor (above), or try classic tools below.
    • 
        
  • Windows: ANSIedit (modern), PabloDraw (classic, free), MadNight’s NFO Viewer.
    • 
        
  • Web: EmojiCombos (ASCII), asciiart.eu (browse/make), TextKool Generator, ManyTools Image2ASCII.
    • 
        
  • Mac/Linux: Any plain-text editor, or Ansilove.
